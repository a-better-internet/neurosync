How NeuroSync Works

NeuroSync plays binaural beats — two pure tones, one in each ear, tuned a few hertz apart. Put on stereo headphones and your mind perceives a third, slower pulse at the difference between them. That pulse is what gently nudges your brain toward a chosen rhythm.

The Idea

Play a tone at 174 Hz on the left and 182 Hz on the right, and the 8 Hz gap becomes an Alpha beat. Choose a state below and NeuroSync sets that gap for you, sliding smoothly whenever you switch.

Brainwave States
The Controls
Play / Pause
The large center button starts and stops the tones. Its glow pulses in time with the current rhythm.
Brainwave Selector
Tap Gamma through Delta to pick a target. The beat glides to the new frequency rather than jumping.
Session Timer
Choose 5, 10, or 15 minutes. In the final half-minute the sound "breathes" with slow swells, then fades to silence.
Solfeggio Carrier Settings
The base tone the beat rides on. Lower carriers feel warmer and less fatiguing over long sessions.
Acoustic Mixer Settings
Balance how prominent the beat is, add brown-noise masking, widen the stereo field, and dial in hall reverb.
Headphones
Essential — the effect only works when each ear receives its own separate channel.
